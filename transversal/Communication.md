# Communication

Summary of a course about communication.

## Interpersonal communication

### The 4 x 20 rule in a first contact
* first impression is difficult to change and could influence greatly in the communication
* 20 steps / actions
  * dress according to business
  * engaged and welcoming posture
  * shake hands frankly and sincerely  
  * check if good visible with webcam
* 20 first words
  * express yourself with a playful tone and greet your customer politely, while introducing yourself  
* 20 cm distance
  * face is important
  * avoid bad odor (body, breath)   
* 20 seconds


### Johari window

* Johari window is a technique that helps people better understand their relationship with 
  themselves and others.
* see [johari window on wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johari_window)


### Life positions

* the life positions refers to the specific behavior towards others that an individual learns on the basis of 
  certain assumptions made very early in the life
* recognize your life positions and those of your interlecutors can help the communication
* see [wikipedia](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analyse_transactionnelle#Positions_de_vie)


### Safeharbour behaviour

* in case of crisis, we react in our safeharbour behaviour (related to life positions)


### Analyze transactionelle

* in a communication, interlocutors put themselve, or the other interlocutor implicitly in a 
  given ego-state: parent, adult or child

see [états du moi (french)](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89tats_du_Moi) and 
[transactions (french)](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analyse_transactionnelle#Transactions)


### Facts, feelings & opinions

* distinguate between
  * fact: verifiable, measurable observations -> undebatable
  * feeling: emotions (what / how i feel) -> undebatable
  * opinions: jugement (my point of view) -> DEBATABLE


### Influence tactics

some influence tactics are:
* *rational persuasion*: the person uses logical arguments and factual evidence to 
  persuade you that a proposal or request is viable and likely to 
  result in the attainment of task objective
* *inspirational appeals*: the person makes a request or proposal that arouses
  enthusiasm by appealing to your values, ideals, and
  aspirations or by increasing your confidence that you can do
  it.
* *consultation tactics*: the person seeks your participation in planning a strategy,
  activity, or change for which support and assistance are
  desired, or the person is willing to modify a proposal to deal
  with your concerns and suggestions
* *personal appeal*: the person appeals to your feelings of loyalty and friendship
  toward him or her before asking you to do something
  
One of the worse influence tactics is rational persuasion (even if used a lot; e.g. Covid-19 period)

Best influence tactics are: inspirational appeals, consultation tactics, personal appeal.

## Communicate

Good communication is well-prepared, there are several aspects to prepare: tactic, mental, material

### Tactic

#### Know the audience

* know your audience by appling [SONCASE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SONCAS)
* what is the public searching?
  * S: Security
  * O: Orgueil / Pride
  * N: Nouveauté / Innovation
  * C: Comfort
  * A: Argent / Money
  * S: Sympathy
  * E: Ethics


#### Know the different stakeholders

* different stakeholders:
 * decider: the one who triggers the decision
 * buyer: the one who sets the conditions of purchase
 * filter: lobbying power
 * executor: the one who will use your service
 * advisor : provides its expertise, technical for example

Known important weight in final decision: +, = or -
Known attitude toward you approach: ally, neutral, hostile


#### Ask & Listen

* Apply [CQQCOQP (french)](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/QQOQCCP) - Qui ? Quoi ? Où ? Quand ? Comment ? Combien ? Pourquoi ?
* don't think instead of the interlocutor, ask him/her


### Framework

Try to tell a story

* a given CONTEXT require a SOLUTION
* by understanding the CONTEXT we can identify a PAIN/CHALLENGE to solve
* the solution can be presented with C.A.P.B:
  * C: Caracteritics -> technical/functional element of the solution
  * A: Advantages -> in comparison to...
  * P: Proof -> demo, references
  * B: Benefits -> advantage to customer


### Material

#### Tips

* work with a storyboard -> what and how communication the message
  * powerpoint provide "storyboard like" feature with "foliensortierung"  
* 1 key information per slide
* 3 bullet points maximum per slide
* use visual bullet point
* use short sentences and keywords
* use icons instead of images
  * use https://thenounproject.com/ to find icons  
* use SUCCESS method
  * S: Simplicity
  * U: Unexpectedness
  * C: Concreteness
  * C: Credibility
  * E: Emotions
  * S: Stories
* show something concrete instead of just telling (demo)
* make it personal (personal experience tell more to people)
* focus something on specific detail / use case to keep focus of audience
